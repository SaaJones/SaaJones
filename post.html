Lab2:Defining and comparing and contrasting 4 kinds of projections

Lab objectives

This Lab aimed to show why different map projections are important in Arc GIS pro and compared the different purposeses and applications of each map projection while also the cartographic properties distorts or preserves, and limitations of each projection.

I created four map projections with the following coordinate reference systems: Goode Homolosine, Mercator, Robinson, and Sinusodial.I obtained this by using data from the Natural Earth data for each map and then changing the coordinate reference system for each map using the coordinate references tab on map properties. Lastly, I displayed each of the four maps using a multi-frame map layout.

Lab 3:US Census Data using spatial mapping

Lab Objectives

This lab described the spatial distribution of the Hispanic population in the U.S. by state in 2010, according to the U.S. Census.

During this lab I first identified the states that had the largest amount and also the smallest amount of Hispanics within their population by using the data given in the 2010 US Census dataset. Using those findings, I then created a choropleth map showing the percentage of Hispanics by state.

>Lab 5: Finding a Location for a new health food market

Lab objectives

The city government is looking to choose a location to develop a new store that will sell healthy food. The good location would fit the folowing criteria, it would be: within a targeted area for investment,easily accessible by public transpotation, and not in range of any current farmers markets or corner stores that have healthy foods.

In order to solve this problem, I started off by buffering the subway and regional rail data to 2000ft then, I combined the data through the union operation and intersected to create the empowerment zone and the regional rail and subway union file for overlapping areas. I also completed the same process for the farmers market and corner stores to 1200ft. Lastly, I erased the farmers market and corner store union features that overlapped with the subway and regional rail union.
